# Customer Journey Map
## Overview
The customer journey map for ml-toolmatch outlines the phases a Series A/B startup goes through when identifying and acquiring the right tools and services for their ML teams. The phases are: Aware, Consider, Try, Adopt, and Expand.

## Aware
* **Trigger Event**: The startup's ML team expresses frustration with current tooling, or a new project requires specialized ML tools.
* **Friction Points**: Lack of knowledge about available ML tools, difficulty in evaluating tool effectiveness.
* **User Emotions**: Frustration, uncertainty.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Provide educational content (blog posts, webinars) highlighting the importance of proper ML tooling, offer a free consultation to discuss ML tooling needs.
* **Metric**: Website traffic, social media engagement (e.g., 500 unique website visitors, 20 social media shares).

## Consider
* **Trigger Event**: The startup begins researching ML tooling options, considering recommendations from peers or online reviews.
* **Friction Points**: Information overload, difficulty in comparing tools, concern about integration with existing infrastructure.
* **User Emotions**: Confusion, skepticism.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Offer a comprehensive guide to ML tooling, provide case studies of successful implementations, offer a free trial or demo of ml-toolmatch.
* **Metric**: Email open rates, guide downloads (e.g., 200 guide downloads, 30% email open rate).

## Try
* **Trigger Event**: The startup decides to try ml-toolmatch, either through a free trial or a paid pilot.
* **Friction Points**: Technical difficulties during onboarding, uncertainty about how to use the platform effectively.
* **User Emotions**: Excitement, apprehension.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Provide dedicated onboarding support, offer personalized recommendations for ML tooling, ensure seamless integration with existing infrastructure.
* **Metric**: Trial conversion rate, customer satisfaction (CSAT) score (e.g., 25% trial conversion rate, CSAT score of 85).

## Adopt
* **Trigger Event**: The startup decides to adopt ml-toolmatch as their ML tooling solution.
* **Friction Points**: Concerns about long-term costs, potential technical issues, need for ongoing support.
* **User Emotions**: Relief, optimism.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Offer customized onboarding, provide regular check-ins and support, ensure ongoing value through platform updates and new feature releases.
* **Metric**: Customer retention rate, net promoter score (NPS) (e.g., 90% customer retention rate, NPS of 40).

## Expand
* **Trigger Event**: The startup achieves success with ml-toolmatch and considers expanding their use of the platform.
* **Friction Points**: Concerns about scalability, need for additional features or support.
* **User Emotions**: Satisfaction, loyalty.
* **Opportunities to Delight**: Offer customized solutions for large-scale deployments, provide priority support, ensure ongoing innovation and platform development.
* **Metric**: Upsell/cross-sell rate, customer lifetime value (CLV) (e.g., 20% upsell/cross-sell rate, CLV of $100,000).
